Every note you create in Anarlog is immediately searchable and available as a plain Markdown file on disk. You can find anything you've written in seconds, export any session to a shareable document, or open your notes in any editor you already use — no lock-in, no proprietary format.

## Full-text search

Anarlog indexes all of your note titles and content using a fast local search engine (powered by Tantivy). To search, press **⌘ K** or click the search icon in the left sidebar. Results appear instantly with highlighted matches as you type.

Search covers:

- Note and session titles
- Your handwritten memos
- AI-generated summaries
- Transcript text

Results are ranked by relevance and support language-aware tokenization, so searching in any of the 45+ supported languages works naturally.

## Export

To export a note, open the session and click the **⋯ More** button in the top-right corner of the note header, then choose **Export**. An export modal appears with the following options:

**File formats**

| Format | Best for |
|---|---|
| **PDF** | Sharing with stakeholders, printing, archiving |
| **Markdown** | Importing into Obsidian, Notion, or any Markdown-aware tool |
| **TXT** | Plain text for pasting anywhere |
| **Org** | Emacs Org-mode workflows |

**Include options** — choose what content to include in the export:

- **Memo** — your handwritten notes from the session
- **Summary** — the AI-generated structured summary
- **Transcript** — the full speech-to-text transcript

Click **Export** and the file is saved to your Downloads folder. Anarlog automatically opens the Downloads folder in Finder after the export completes.

## Your notes as plain files

Anarlog stores every note as a `.md` (Markdown) file inside your vault folder. You can open, edit, or move these files with any application:

## Syncing across devices

Because your notes are just files in a folder, you can use any file sync service to keep them in sync across your Macs or back them up automatically:

- **iCloud Drive** — place your vault inside `~/Library/Mobile Documents/` or any iCloud-synced folder
- **Dropbox / Google Drive / OneDrive** — move your vault into the synced folder
- **Syncthing** — peer-to-peer sync with no cloud intermediary
- **Git** — version-control your notes with a full commit history

<Tip>
  To use Anarlog notes directly inside Obsidian, go to **Settings → Storage** and set your vault path to an existing Obsidian vault folder. Every meeting note you create will appear in Obsidian immediately, complete with Markdown formatting and AI summaries.
</Tip>
