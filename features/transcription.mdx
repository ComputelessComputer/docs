As soon as you start recording, Anarlog begins transcribing in real time. Words stream into the **Transcript** tab of your note as they are spoken, so you always have a live text view of the conversation alongside your own notes. When the session ends, the final transcript is saved permanently with your note.

## Transcription modes

You can choose between local on-device transcription and a range of cloud providers depending on your privacy requirements and accuracy needs.

<Tip>
  Local transcription keeps your audio 100% on-device from capture to text — nothing is ever uploaded to a server. If privacy is your top priority, choose the **Whisper Large V3** model in **Settings → Transcription**.
</Tip>

For step-by-step instructions on configuring each cloud provider, see [Transcription Providers](/ai/transcription-providers).

## Supported languages

Anarlog supports transcription in 45+ languages through Whisper and the cloud providers. Select your preferred spoken language in **Settings → General → Language**. Some providers also support automatic language detection and live code-switching between languages within the same session.

If you speak a language that a provider cannot handle, Anarlog automatically falls back to the closest supported language so transcription continues without interruption.

## The Transcript tab

While a session is active or after it ends, click the **Transcript** tab in the note header to view the full transcript. During a live session, you'll see animated waveform sticks indicating that audio is being captured and words are streaming in. The tab shows a spinner while a batch transcription job is processing after the meeting.

Right-clicking the Transcript tab gives you quick actions:

- **Copy** — copies the full transcript text to your clipboard
- **Regenerate** — re-runs transcription on the saved audio file using your current settings
- **Delete recording** — removes the audio file while keeping the transcript

## Speaker diarization

Several cloud providers support speaker diarization — attributing each spoken segment to a distinct speaker label. When diarization is enabled by your provider, speaker names (or labels like "Speaker 1", "Speaker 2") appear inline in the transcript view. Provider support for diarization varies; check the [Transcription Providers](/ai/transcription-providers) page for details on which providers offer it and how to enable it.
