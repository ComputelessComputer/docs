Anarlog uses the **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** — an open standard developed by Anthropic — as part of the technology that powers its built-in AI assistant. When you ask Anarlog a question about your notes in the chat panel, MCP is the layer that lets the AI discover and read the relevant content from your local vault.

<Info>
  MCP is an open protocol that standardizes how AI systems connect to external data sources and tools. Learn more at [modelcontextprotocol.io](https://modelcontextprotocol.io).
</Info>

## How MCP works inside Anarlog

Anarlog's AI assistant runs entirely on your Mac. When you ask it a question, it uses MCP-based tools to:

- **Search your meeting notes** by keywords, date, participants, or topic
- **Read full note content** including structured sections and inline text
- **Access transcripts** linked to a meeting
- **Look up contacts and calendar events** to answer questions about people and meetings
- **Search the web** for public information that complements your notes

All of this happens locally. Your notes are not sent to any third party by Anarlog itself; only the content the AI includes in its reply to a language model is transmitted to the AI provider you have configured.

## The built-in AI assistant

To use AI features, open any note and click the chat panel, or use the global chat. You can ask questions like:

- *"What did we decide about the API design in last Tuesday's engineering sync?"*
- *"Summarize the action items from my last three calls with Acme Corp."*
- *"Who attended the product review on Monday?"*

The assistant searches your local vault and returns answers grounded in your actual notes.

## AI provider settings

Anarlog's AI assistant connects to a language model of your choice. Open **Settings → AI** to configure which provider and model to use. Anarlog supports both cloud-hosted models and local models running on your Mac.

<Note>
  When the AI assistant reads your notes to answer a question, that note content is included in the context sent to whichever AI provider you have configured. Review your AI provider's privacy policy if this is a concern. Anarlog itself never stores or forwards your note content to third parties.
</Note>

## Connecting external MCP clients

Anarlog does not currently expose a standalone MCP server endpoint for external AI clients such as Claude Desktop or Cursor. The MCP tooling in Anarlog is internal to the application.

If external MCP connectivity is added in a future release, connection details will appear in **Settings** and this page will be updated with verified setup instructions.
