Anarlog is an open-source, local-first AI meeting notetaker built for macOS. It captures your meetings — whether on Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, a phone call, or in person — and turns them into plain Markdown files saved on your own machine. There are no bots, no cloud pipelines, and no accounts required. If you want full control over your meeting notes without sacrificing the convenience of AI-generated summaries, Anarlog is for you.

## How it works

Anarlog follows a straightforward three-step flow from the moment a meeting starts to the moment your notes are ready:

1. **Record** — Anarlog captures both your microphone input and system audio directly on your Mac, so every voice in the room or on the call is picked up without sending a bot into the meeting.
2. **Transcribe** — The audio is transcribed on-device using a local model, or you can choose a cloud provider such as Deepgram, AssemblyAI, or OpenAI. Either way, you decide where the audio goes.
3. **Summarize** — Once the meeting ends, click **Enhance** and Anarlog uses your chosen AI model to generate a structured summary. Notes are written back to the same Markdown file, ready to read, search, or sync.

## What makes Anarlog different

Most meeting notetakers are cloud services that store your recordings on someone else's server. Anarlog takes the opposite approach: every file lives on your disk, every model runs under your API keys, and the application itself is open source under the MIT license so you can inspect, fork, or self-host every line of it.

There are no accounts to create and no usage-based billing hidden behind a free tier. You bring your own LLM keys, or you run a local model like Ollama and pay nothing at all. Your notes are already in a format — plain Markdown — that will outlast any proprietary app.

Because Anarlog is open source, the community can audit exactly how audio is captured, how transcription works, and where data moves. That transparency is the foundation of the privacy-first promise: it is not a policy you have to trust, it is code you can read.

<Note>
  Anarlog is a macOS-only application. Both Apple Silicon (aarch64) and Intel (x86_64) Macs are supported. Linux and Windows builds are not planned at this time.
</Note>
