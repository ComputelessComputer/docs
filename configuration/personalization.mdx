Generic transcription models are trained on broad datasets, which means they often stumble on the specific vocabulary that makes your work unique — the colleague with the unusual name, the internal product codename, the three-letter acronym your team uses a hundred times a day. The **Personalization** settings let you build a custom dictionary that teaches Anarlog to recognize these terms, so they appear correctly in your transcripts from the very first mention.

## How the dictionary works

When you add a term to your dictionary, Anarlog passes the full list as hints to the transcription model before each session. The model uses your terms as preferred spellings when it encounters phonetically similar audio, steering it away from common misspellings toward the exact form you specified.

## Adding terms

1. Open **Settings → Personalization**.
2. Click in the input field and type a word or phrase — a person's name, a product name, a technical term, or an acronym.
3. Press <kbd>Enter</kbd> or click **Add**.
4. The term appears in your dictionary list and is applied to all future transcriptions.

You can add multiple terms at once by separating them with commas in the input field.

<Tip>
  Start with your own name and the names of the teammates you meet with most often. Names are the most frequent source of transcription errors in meeting tools, and fixing them has an outsized impact on how readable your notes are.
</Tip>

## Good candidates for your dictionary

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **People's names** | Saoirse, Laszlo, Priyanka, Wojciech |
| **Product names** | your app's name, internal codenames, version identifiers |
| **Company names** | clients, partners, vendors with unusual spellings |
| **Technical jargon** | framework names, API names, domain-specific terminology |
| **Acronyms** | internal abbreviations your team uses regularly |

## Removing terms

To remove a term, hover over it in the dictionary list and click the **circle-minus** icon that appears on the right side of the row. The term is removed immediately and will no longer be used in future transcriptions.

You can also search your existing terms by typing in the input field — the list filters in real time so you can quickly find and remove a specific entry.

<Note>
  Dictionary terms are provided as hints to the transcription model; they do not guarantee perfect recognition. The degree of accuracy improvement depends on the transcription provider you have selected and the phonetic similarity between your terms and the surrounding speech. Uncommon but phonetically distinct words benefit the most.
</Note>
