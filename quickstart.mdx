---
title: "Get Started with Anarlog in 5 Minutes"
sidebarTitle: "Quickstart"
description: "Download Anarlog, grant audio permissions, connect an AI provider, and record your first meeting note in under five minutes."
---

This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a fresh download to a saved, AI-summarized meeting note. The whole process takes under five minutes, and you can start capturing transcripts right away even before you connect an AI provider.

<Note>
  Anarlog runs on macOS only — Apple Silicon (aarch64) and Intel (x86_64) are both supported. Linux and Windows are not available.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Anarlog">
    Go to the [latest release on GitHub](https://github.com/fastrepl/anarlog/releases/latest) and download the build that matches your Mac:

    - **Apple Silicon (M1, M2, M3, M4)** — download the `aarch64` `.dmg` file.
    - **Intel Mac** — download the `x86_64` `.dmg` file.

    Not sure which chip you have? Click the Apple menu → **About This Mac**. The processor line will say either "Apple M…" or "Intel".
  </Step>

  <Step title="Grant audio permissions">
    Anarlog needs two macOS permissions before it can capture any audio:

    - **Microphone** — lets Anarlog hear your voice.
    - **Screen Recording** — lets Anarlog access system audio from Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and other apps. macOS gates system audio behind this permission.

    On first launch the onboarding screen will prompt you for both. Click **Allow access** for each. If macOS asks for confirmation in a system dialog, click **Allow** there too. Anarlog moves to the next step automatically once both permissions are granted.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect an AI provider">
    Open **Settings → Intelligence** to choose how Anarlog transcribes and summarizes your meetings.

    - **Transcription provider** — select a local model (Whisper, runs entirely on-device) or a cloud service such as Deepgram, AssemblyAI, or OpenAI. Paste your API key into the field provided.
    - **LLM provider** — select the model that generates your AI summaries. Options include OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini, OpenRouter, Ollama, LM Studio, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint. For a fully local and free setup, choose **Ollama** and point it at a running Ollama instance on your machine.

    <Tip>
      You can skip this step entirely and use Anarlog for transcription only. Recording and saving transcripts works without any AI provider configured. You can add a provider later whenever you're ready to generate summaries.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first meeting note">
    Once onboarding is complete, you'll see the main Anarlog window:

    1. In the left sidebar, click the **+** button to create a new session. Give it a name, or let Anarlog fill it in from your calendar.
    2. Join your meeting (or start one) in whatever app you normally use — Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, a FaceTime call, or just a conversation in the same room.
    3. Click the **Listen** button (the microphone icon) in the Anarlog toolbar. The waveform animation confirms that audio capture is active.
    4. Talk. Anarlog transcribes in real time and displays the rolling transcript in the editor panel.
    5. When the meeting ends, click **Listen** again to stop recording.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate your AI summary">
    After recording stops, your transcript is already saved as a Markdown file on disk. To generate a structured AI summary:

    1. Click the **Enhance** button at the top of the editor panel.
    2. Anarlog sends the transcript to your configured LLM and writes the summary back into the same file — headings, action items, and key points included.
    3. The finished note is a standard `.md` file. Open it in any Markdown editor, sync it with iCloud or Dropbox, or commit it to a git repository.

    That's it — your first Anarlog meeting note is ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>
